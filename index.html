Why Do We Need To Counsume Responsible ?

Responsible consumption involves being mindful of the environmental impact of our consumption habits. Many products we consume have a significant carbon footprint, contribute to pollution, deforestation, habitat destruction, and other environmental issues.

Consuming responsibly is crucial for protecting the environment, conserving resources, promoting social justice, improving health and well-being, and ensuring economic sustainability. By making mindful choices about what we consume and how we consume it, we can work towards a more sustainable and equitable world for current and future generations.

What If ? We Do Not Consume Responsible.

Resource Waste

When consumption and production aren't managed effectively, it can lead to the wastage of resources like water, energy, and raw materials.

Environmental Pollution

Improper disposal of daily use items can contribute to environmental pollution through littering and improper waste management practices.

Decline in Productivity

Inefficient production processes or inadequate consumption planning can lead to a decrease in productivity within industries or households.

Economic Instability

Economic instability arises when there is a lack of balance or predictability in economic conditions. Mismanagement in consumption and production can significantly contribute to this instability.

Health Hazards

Mismanagement in daily item use, including inadequate disposal or usage practices, can pose significant health risks to individuals and communities.

Our Solution

These are some solutions on how to consume and recycle some major waste producing items used in daily life.

Plastic Water Bottle

Waste Type:Plastic Waste

Responsible Consumption:

Opt for reusable water bottles made from materials like stainless steel or glass to reduce plastic waste.

Decomposition:

If recycling facilities are available, ensure proper disposal in recycling bins. If not, consider repurposing bottles for crafts or upcycling projects to extend their lifespan.

Disposable Coffee Cups

Waste Type:Mixed plastic and paper

Responsible Consumption:

Use reusable coffee mugs or bring your own travel mug to coffee shops to minimize single-use cup waste.

Decomposition:

While paper components can be composted, the plastic lining makes traditional decomposition challenging. Look for specialized composting facilities that can handle mixed waste or consider alternative uses for the cups.

Smartphone And Electronics

Waste Type:Electronic waste

Responsible Consumption:

Extend the lifespan of electronics by repairing and upgrading when possible. Consider purchasing refurbished devices or participating in buy-back programs.

Decomposition:

Take electronics to designated e-waste recycling centers or drop-off locations to ensure proper disposal and recycling of valuable components. Avoid throwing electronics in regular trash bins due to hazardous materials.

Disposable Plastic Cutlery

Waste Type:Plastic Waste

Responsible Consumption:

Choose reusable cutlery sets made from sustainable materials like bamboo or stainless steel for meals on-the-go.

Decomposition:

Look for recycling programs that accept plastic cutlery or consider repurposing them for non-food-related tasks like gardening or arts and crafts. Avoid composting unless the cutlery is certified as compostable.

Expired And Unused Medications

Waste Type:Pharmaceutical Waste

Responsible Consumption:

Only acquire medications as needed and avoid overstocking to minimize waste. Consult with healthcare professionals for proper medication management.

Decomposition:

Follow local guidelines for medication disposal, which may include take-back programs, pharmacy drop-off locations, or medication mail-back services. Avoid flushing medications down the toilet or throwing them in the trash to prevent environmental contamination.
